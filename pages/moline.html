History of Minneapolis-Moline

  • Minneapolis-Moline was formed by a merger between Minneapolis Steel & Machinery, Twin City Tractors, Minneapolis Threshing Machine and Moline Plow. The merger saw factories in both Minneapolis, MN and Moline, IL; hence the name.

    1929

  • The 'U Series' tractors begin production. Minneapolis-Moline pioneered the closed-cab tractor concept with the UDLX Comfortractor, also known as the Model U Deluxe. The model had bodywork as well as a fully-integrated all-weather cabin with a passenger seat. With a price tag of $2,155, almost double that of John Deere and Farmall, and the advent of a recession, only 125 UDLX models were sold.

    1938

  • Minneapolis-Moline UDLX

  • Minneapolis-Moline introduced the GT tractor. The 4-cylinder 40HP tractor had a five plow rating as well a 5 speed transmission.

    1946

    1946

  • Minneapolis-Moline GTA

  • Minneapolis-Moline produces the 'Z Series' tractors. These tractors featured more comforts including the seat and improved steering. The tractors were available in a variety of configurations including tricycle, wide front and standard. The tractors had a 35HP engine with 6-speed transmission.

    1953

  • Minneapolis-Moline introduces the 'G Series' tractors. These tractors would be the most powerful tractors that the company would produce. The G-1350 was powered by a 6-cylinder diesel and would produce 141HP. It was driven by a transmission with ten forward and two reverse gears.

    1959

  • Minneapolis-Moline ZB

  • Throughout the 60's, Minneapolis-Moline produced the 'M Series' tractors. Most notably, the M-604 which was 4-wheel drive.

    1960

  • Minneapolis-Moline is acquired by the White Motor Company, who owns the Oliver tractor brand.

    1963

  • Minneapolis-Moline M-602

  • In the early 70's, White produced four Oliver models rebranded as Minneapolis-Moline before dropping the brand name completely in 1974.

    1974

  • In 1985, White Motor Company was placed in involuntary bankruptcy and is acquired by AGCO in 1991.
