History of John Deere

  • John Deere, born in 1804, opened a shop in Grand Detour, Illinois where he intended to work as a repairman for the village. Deere saw a need to produce a new type of plow, one that would prevent the rich midwestern soil from becoming stuck. He fashioned a new plow from a steel saw blade and thus became the beginnings of John Deere in the agricultural business.

    1837

  • John Deere's work began to increase in popularity due to an innovative business model that saw John Deere produce plows before an order had been placed. This caused sales to rise exponentially as farmers could purchases and begin using a plow in the same day. John Deere moved operations to Moline, Illinois in 1848 to gain access to the railroad and build a new factory where they would increase yearly plow production from 100 in 1842 to over 1000 in 1857. John Deere and his stakeholders incorporated as Deere & Company in 1868, with John's son Charles as the primary stakeholder. Production of farm implements expanded to wagons, planters and cultivators through to the beginning of the 20th century.

    1842 - 1900

  • First Production Tractor

  • By the turn of the 20th century, Deere & Company was under pressure to move into the tractor business. Research and development began on an all-wheel drive tricycle tractor under the supervision of John Dain. This led to Deere & Company's first production tractor, the Dain All-Wheel Drive. Original sale price $700.

    1916

  • Deere & Company purchases Waterloo Gasoline Engine Company who was producing the popular tractor, the Waterloo Boy. The companies founder, John Froelich, was credited with invention of the first gas powered tractor in the late 1800's.

    1918

  • The Waterloo Boy

  • Deere & Company produce the first tractor under the brand John Deere, the Model D. The tractor was a hit with farmers in the midwest, boasting 27 horsepower and almost 2000 pounds lighter than its predecessor. Later models replaced the steel wheels with rubber to add comfort and safety. The Model D still holds the longest production run, from 1923 - 1953, originally selling for $1000

    1923

  • John Deere produces its first combine harvester. The 35 HP unit was available with a 12 or 16 foot platform.

    1927

  • Model D

  • The Great Depression hits. John Deere takes on $12 million in farmers notes and extends terms on already purchased tractors. Sales fell sharply between 1931 and 1932, however, strong customer loyalty resulted.

    1931

  • Despite difficult times, John Deere produces the Model A and Model B. There were 8 variations which included tricycle, hi-crop and orchard.

    1934

  • Model B

  • With the United States entry into WWII, John Deere begins manufacturing military tractors, airplane parts and ammunition. A John Deere Battalion was created which was comprised of John Deere employees who were sent to France and Belgium to repair tanks.

    1943

  • John Deere produces the first diesel tractor, the Model R. The R was the first model with a 'live' independent PTO and also featured independent hydraulics.

    1950

  • Model R

  • The New Generation of John Deere Tractors is released. The new tractors featured styling by Henry Dreyfuss and would become the standard by which all tractors were measured. In 1962, the 5010 became the first tractor to produce 100 HP at the drawbar and PTO.

    1961

  • John Deere releases the Generation II tractors which featured the first truly successful cab. The cabs were well designed and reduced noise, vibrations and protected from outside temperatures. The tractors also added to the transmission by adding high and low gears to each range.

    1972

  • John Deere 4440

  • The Generation III tractor is released. The series 5000 tractors begin production as well as a new compact utility tractor that featured a 3 cylinder diesel engine.

    1990

  • John Deere heavily incorporates software into their tractors. Guidance systems allow farmers to become more efficient in all areas of farming. In 2016, John Deere begins experiments with fully electric tractors as well as applying machine learning to further optimize farming.

    2000's
