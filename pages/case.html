History of Case

  • J.I. Case founds Racine Threshing Machine Works in Racine, Wisconsin. The company produces a thresher based on an innovative design for separating straw and grain.

    1842

  • Case begins manufacturing steam engines and builds the first steam tractor. The tractor is mounted on wheels but is still drawn by horses and only used to power other machines. By 1886 Case becomes the largest manufacturer of steam engines in the world.

    1869

  • Horse Drawn Steam Tractor

  • Case builds a gasoline-powered tractor. However, the market is not ready for the transition from steam, so Case waits until 1911 to reintroduce it.

    1892

  • By the turn of the century, steam is still the preferred source of power. Case produces a 110HP tractor that weighed up to 42,500lbs when loaded with coal and water. The tractor produced over 3000 ft-lbs of torque at 240 rpm and could pull up to a 12 bottom plow. Though very popular opposed to conventional horsepower, few farmers could afford the $3,600 price tag.

    1901

  • Case Steam Tractor

  • The advent of the oil engine began to signal a change on the horizon, Case begins to focus on gasoline and oil engines. They hire race car driver Joe Jagersberger, who tests an engine by racing it in the Indianapolis 500. They begin manufacturing a series of kerosene tractors similar to the Rumely Oil Pull. Steam still remains part of the Case business model.

    1911

  • Interest began to move from steam to the internal combustion engine in favour of a lighter and more agile tractor. Though the tractors were less powerful and could pull only 2 or 3 bottom plows, they could move much faster and were much more affordable. Case begins production of the 'Crossmotor Series' which featured various models from 20 - 60 horsepower.

    1916

  • Case 10-18

  • Case ceases production of its legendary steam engines entirely. In the same year, it begins production of the 'Letter Series' tractors. The C and L models were released in 1929 followed by the D, S, V and LA in the early 40's, which featured variations for row crop and orchard.

    1928

  • Case produces its first diesel tractor, the 500. The 6-cylinder engine becomes well respected as well as the tractor itself, which featured an independent live PTO, hydraulics and power steering; a luxury during the era.

    1953

  • Case 500 Diesel

  • Case begins producing the 'B Series' tractors. The models featured more configurations for different farming applications as well larger and more powerful tractors. The B series tractors moved away from the iconic Flambeau Red paint color in favour of a 2-tone paint scheme of Desert Sun and Power Red.

    1958

  • The 'B Series' tractors were quickly replaced by the '30 Series' tractors in favour of high horsepower and better transmissions. Case also introduces its first 4-wheel drive tractor. Weighing close to 17,000lbs and with a cost of only $20,000, it became highly popular among farmers.

    1960

  • Case 1030 Comfort King

  • Case begins the 70's with the '70 Series' tractor, which would become a highly popular model and the backbone of sales throughout the decade. The series was topped by the Model 2670, which produced 219HP at the drawbar.

    1969

  • The parent company of Case buys Interational Harvester. The company is originally branded Case International but later moves to Case IH. The move makes Case IH the second largest farm equipment manufacturer in the world.

    1985

  • Case IH Magnum 7140

  • Case IH launches two new series under the combined engineering of Case and International Harvester. The Magnum and Maxxum tractors were praised for their versatility as a multi-purpose tractor with high performance and serviceability.

    1988

  • After acquiring tractor manufacturer Steiger, Case IH releases the Quadtrac tractor. The 360HP 4-wheel drive features tracks instead of wheels, which reduce ground compaction and increase traction.

    1997

  • Case IH Autonomous Concept Tractor

  • Case IH launches the continuously variable transmission (CVT) technology in its tractors. The CVT automatically adjusts to constantly deliver the best possible balance of power and fuel efficiency at field speeds.

    2009

  • Case IH unveils an autonomous concept tractor. The concept proves popular among farmers who are finding more difficulty in running farming operations with less manpower.

    2016
